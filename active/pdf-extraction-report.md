### 5. SORA VIDEO PROMPT STRUCTURE SYSTEM GUIDE
**Pages:** 16 | **Key Frameworks:**

**Master Template Structure:**

**1. Header Section**
```
[PROJECT TITLE] - [DURATION]
Style: [Comprehensive style description including:
  - Video format & aspect ratio
  - Camera device/equipment
  - Overall aesthetic & tone
  - Color grading approach
  - Pacing style
  - Target platform optimization]
```

**Example:** "Sora Video Prompt - TikTok Skincare UGC (10 seconds)
Style: Authentic TikTok UGC aesthetic, iPhone 15 Pro vertical video (9:16), natural lighting with bright, clean exposure, fast-paced editing with quick cuts typical of viral skincare content. Minimal color grading — slight warmth in highlights, clean whites, natural skin texture preserved. Energetic, relatable, Gen-Z optimized pacing."

**2. Shot-by-Shot Breakdown**
```
Shot [NUMBER]: [SHOT NAME] ([START TIME]-[END TIME])
Location: [Detailed location description including:
  - Physical setting
  - Key environmental elements
  - Lighting sources present
  - Spatial details]
  
[Character/Subject description including:
  - Physical appearance details
  - Wardrobe
  - Positioning
  - Emotional state]
  
Cinematography:
  - Camera: [Movement type, lens behavior, shooting style]
  - Framing: [Aspect ratio, shot size, composition percentage, angle]
  - Depth of field: [Focus characteristics, sharp vs blurred]
  - Lighting: [Key light sources, direction, quality, color temperature]
  - Mood: [Emotional tone, atmosphere, viewer feeling]
  
Actions:
  - [timestamp range] [Specific, granular action description]
  - [timestamp range] [Next specific action]
  - [Include micro-movements: eyebrow raises, blinks, finger taps]
  
Dialogue:
  [Character name] ([delivery style, tone, energy level]): "[Exact spoken words]"
  
Background Sound:
  [List all ambient sounds, foley elements, object interactions]
```

**Detailed Component Breakdown:**

**Style Section Requirements:**
1. Video Format: 9:16, 16:9, etc.
2. Capture Device: Specific camera or phone model for realism
3. Visual Aesthetic: UGC, cinematic, documentary, commercial, etc.
4. Lighting Approach: General lighting philosophy
5. Color Treatment: Grading style & color palette
6. Pacing: Edit rhythm (fast-paced, slow-contemplative, etc.)
7. Platform Context: TikTok-native, Instagram Reel, YouTube, etc.

**Location Description Requirements:**
1. Primary Setting: Specific room/environment type
2. Key Set Pieces: Important objects, furniture, environmental elements
3. Lighting Sources: Windows, lights, practical lights
4. Spatial Details: Size, layout, notable characteristics
5. Atmosphere: Environmental qualities (bright, cramped, airy, etc.)

**Character/Subject Description Requirements:**
1. Age Range: Specific or approximate
2. Physical Features: Distinctive characteristics
3. Ethnicity/Origin: If relevant to creative vision
4. Hair: Style, color, length, state
5. Wardrobe: Specific clothing items, colors, style
6. Skin/Makeup: Bare, natural, made-up, textured, glowing, etc.
7. Positioning: Where they are in relation to camera & environment
8. Emotional State: Current mood, energy, expression

**Cinematography Section Requirements:**

**Camera:**
- Movement type (handheld, static, dolly, push-in, pan, tilt, etc.)
- Lens behavior or focal length equivalent
- Shooting style characteristics (shaky, smooth, professional, amateur, etc.)
- POV characteristics (eye level, slight upward, overhead, etc.)

**Framing:**
- Aspect ratio (must match overall video format)
- Shot size (ECU, CU, MCU, MS, MWS, WS, EWS)
- Composition percentage (e.g., "subject fills 70% of frame")
- Angle (eye level, low angle, high angle, dutch angle, etc.)

**Depth of Field:**
- Focus characteristics (shallow, deep, moderate)
- What should be sharp vs blurred
- How background renders

**Lighting:**
- Key light source & direction
- Fill light sources
- Practical lights in scene
- Light quality (soft, hard, diffused, direct)
- Color temperature (warm, cool, mixed)
- Specific lighting ratios or contrast levels

**Mood:**
- Emotional tone of the shot
- Atmosphere viewer should feel
- Atmospheric descriptors (intimate, energetic, tense, playful, etc.)

**Actions Section Rules:**
1. Break down every second or half-second of movement
2. Use specific, visible actions (not internal states)
3. Include micro-movements (eyebrow raises, blinks, finger taps)
4. Specify direction of movement (upward, toward camera, left to right)
5. Note timing of gestures relative to dialogue
6. Include object interactions with detail (picks up, places down, unscrew, tap, etc.)
7. Describe facial expressions in action terms (eyes widen, mouth forms smile, etc.)
8. Note timing of beats relative to dialogue

**Good Action Examples:**
- [0-0.5s] She raises eyebrows and leans in quickly toward camera
- [2.5-3s] Finger taps phone screen showing "before" photo
- [8-9.5s] Taps jar twice with her finger for emphasis

**Bad Action Examples:**
- She feels excited (internal state, not visible)
- She moves (too vague)
- She does something with the product (not specific enough)

**Dialogue Section Requirements:**
1. Speaker identification
2. Vocal delivery (enthusiastic, rapid, confident, whispered, etc.)
3. Tone/energy level (excited, sarcastic, authoritative, gentle, etc.)
4. Exact spoken words
5. Natural, conversational language
6. Match dialogue length to shot duration
7. Use proper punctuation for delivery cues (em dashes, ellipses, caps for emphasis)

**Dialogue Examples:**
```
Woman (speaking rapidly, excited tone): "Okay so this happened in TWO weeks—"
Woman (voiceover, emphatic): "-and my skin actually DRINKS it."
Woman (speaking directly to camera, enthusiastic and genuine): "Link's in my bio if you wanna try it. Trust me on this one!"
```

**Background Sound Section Requirements:**
1. Ambient Sounds: Room tone, environmental audio
2. Foley Elements: Specific action sounds (jar clicks, fabric rustles)
3. Movement Sounds: Footsteps, hand movements, breathing
4. Object Interactions: Product sounds, surface contacts
5. Relative Volume: If important (soft, prominent, subtle)
6. Sound Quality: ASMR-quality, natural, muffled, crisp, etc.

**Background Sound Example:**
```
Quiet bathroom ambience, fabric rustle, cream texture sound (soft, creamy), gentle skin application (ASMR quality), satisfied breath, natural room ambience
```

**Final Technical Specs Section (Required):**
```
Overall Technical Specs:
- Total duration: [X seconds]
- Format: [Aspect ratio] [resolution]
- Total cuts: [Number] distinct shots
- Pacing: [Description of edit rhythm]
- Color palette: [3-5 primary colors and their context]
- Continuity: [Notes on maintaining consistency across shots]
Audio Mix:
- Detailed audio philosophy including:
  - Music vs. diegetic sound approach
  - Voice levels and clarity (LUFS if relevant)
  - Sound effect levels relative to dialogue
  - Overall aesthetic (music vs. diegetic, ASMR quality, natural soundscape, etc.)
```

**Writing Best Practices:**

**DO:**
- Use specific, concrete details over vague descriptions
- Break actions into granular, timed beats
- Specify camera movements and framing precisely
- Include both technical and emotional/atmospheric elements
- Maintain consistent character descriptions across shots
- Write natural, speakable dialogue
- Consider the platform and format (TikTok requires different pacing than YouTube)
- Include sound design details
- Specify lighting sources and quality

**DON'T:**
- Use vague terms like "beautiful," "nice," "good"
- Skip timestamp breakdowns in actions
- Write dialogue that's too long for the shot duration
- Forget to include continuity elements (lighting, wardrobe, props)
- Change character descriptions between shots
- Use overly technical jargon without context
- Omit sound design elements
- Write internal thoughts or feelings (only visible actions)
- Forget to specify aspect ratio and format

**Quick Reference: Shot Types**

**Framing:**
- Extreme Close-Up (ECU): Face detail, single feature
- Close-Up (CU): Head & shoulders
- Medium Close-Up (MCU): Chest up
- Medium Shot (MS): Waist up
- Medium Wide Shot (MWS): Knees up
- Wide Shot (WS): Full body
- Extreme Wide Shot (EWS): Subject in environment

**Camera Movement:**
- Static: No movement
- Handheld: Natural shake & movement
- Push-in: Moving toward subject
- Pull-out: Moving away from subject
- Pan: Horizontal rotation
- Tilt: Vertical rotation
- Dolly: Camera moves on track
- Tracking: Following subject movement
- Overhead: Top-down perspective
- Dutch Angle: Tilted horizon

**Angles:**
- Eye level: Neutral straight on
- Low angle: Camera below subject, looking up
- High angle: Camera above subject, looking down
- Dutch angle: Tilted horizon

**Example Timestamp Format:**
```
[00:00-00:02] Action description with dialogue if applicable
[00:02-00:04] [Next action and dialogue]
[00:04-00:06] [Continue precisely]
```

**Key Reminders:**
1. Every shot is a distinct unit with its own complete set of details
2. Timestamps are non-negotiable—every action needs precise timing
3. Continuity matters—maintain consistent character descriptions across shots
4. Sound design is equal to visual—never skip audio details
5. Specificity wins—concrete details generate better results than vague descriptions
6. Platform matters—format and pacing should match intended use
7. Natural delivery—dialogue should sound like real speech, not written text
8. Technical + Emotional—balance precise specs with mood & feeling
