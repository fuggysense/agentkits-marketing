# Sales Learnings

Consolidated from sales-enabler, lead-qualifier, and upsell-maximizer agent learnings.

## Sales Enablement

### Confirmed Patterns

- Closing rate benchmark: 5-15% (varies by price point)
- Closing rate is the final high-leverage point in the ad funnel — doubling it (5% -> 10%) halves CPA and doubles profit
- Presentation quality directly drives closing rate — fix presentation before blaming ads or traffic
- Backend offers ($18k-$20k) added to front-end ($3k) can double average client value with zero extra ad spend

### Diagnostic Rules

- Good show-ups but low sales -> Fix presentation, offer structure, or closing sequence (not ads)
- Closing rate below 5% on $3k offer -> Presentation/offer mismatch with audience expectations
- Profitable campaign but margins thin -> Add backend offer to increase LTV, not more ad spend

### Mistakes Not to Repeat

- Blaming ads for low sales when the problem is the presentation or offer
- Not having a backend offer — leaves massive revenue on the table
- Asking agency to "fix ads" when closing rate is the bottleneck

## Upsell & Revenue Expansion

### Confirmed Patterns

- Backend revenue is the real profit multiplier — front-end events are lead gen mechanisms
- Backend conversion rate benchmark: 5-10%
- Adding $18k-$20k backend to $3k front-end doubles average profit per client with zero extra ad spend
- Quarterly LTV growth strategy: add one new upsell/cross-sell product every quarter
- Example progression: $3k front-end -> 1-on-1 coaching -> mini mastermind -> high-ticket program
- Higher LTV = more you can spend to acquire clients = outcompete everyone on ad spend

### Diagnostic Rules

- If LTV is only front-end price -> Missing backend revenue, biggest untapped profit lever
- If competitor outspends you on ads -> Their LTV is higher, you need backend offers to compete
- Profitable front-end but not scaling -> Backend offer allows aggressive scaling by increasing what each client is worth

### Mistakes Not to Repeat

- Treating front-end revenue as the whole business — backend is where margins live
- Not introducing new offers quarterly — LTV should grow over time, not stay flat
- Trying to compete on ad spend alone without increasing LTV

## Reference Data

- Closing rate benchmark: 5-15% (varies by price point and offer)
- Backend conversion rate: 5-10%
- Formula: `Avg Client Value = Front-End Price + (Backend Price x Backend Conversion Rate)`
- Example: $3k + ($20k x 10%) = $5k avg client value
- Doubling LTV from $3k to $6k = can spend 2x to acquire same client and still win
