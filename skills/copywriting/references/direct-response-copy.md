# Direct Response Copy — Master Reference

> Source: The Boring Marketer (thevibemarketer.com). Frameworks from Schwartz, Hopkins, Ogilvy, Halbert, Caples, Sugarman, Collier.
> Load this reference when writing any persuasive copy: landing pages, emails, ads, headlines, CTAs, social posts.

## Core Principle

Write like you're explaining to a smart friend who's skeptical but curious. Back up every claim with specifics. Make the transformation viscerally clear.

---

## Headlines (80% of the Work)

One headline can outpull another by 19.5x. Same product, same offer.

### Master Formula
> **[Action verb] + [specific outcome] + [timeframe or contrast]**
- "Ship your startup in days, not weeks"
- "Save 4 hours per person every single week"

### Headline Types

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Story | "They [doubted] when I [action]... But when I [result]..." | "They Laughed When I Sat Down at the Piano..." |
| Specificity | [Specific number] + [unexpected detail] | "At 60 mph, the loudest noise comes from the electric clock" |
| Question | "Do you [common struggle]?" | "Do You Make These Mistakes in English?" |
| Transformation | "From [bad state] to [good state]" | "From Broke Musician to $100K/Year Music Teacher" |
| How-To | "How to [outcome] without [pain]" | "How to Win Friends and Influence People" |
| Number | "[Number] [ways/tips] to [outcome]" | "7 Copywriting Mistakes That Kill Conversions" |
| Contrarian | "[Common belief] Is Wrong" | "Everything You Know About SEO Is Wrong" |
| Warning | "Don't [action] until [condition]" | "Don't Write Another Ad Until You Read This" |
| Reason-Why | "Why [surprising statement]" | "Why Your Ads Aren't Working" |
| Secret | "The Secret of [desirable outcome]" | "The Secret of Making People Like You" |

### What Makes Headlines Fail
- Clever instead of clear
- No self-interest (what's in it for them?)
- Vague claims instead of specific benefits
- No curiosity gap

## Opening Lines

First sentence has ONE job: get them to read the second.

| Type | Example | Why It Works |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Direct challenge | "You've been using Claude wrong." | Stops scroll, creates tension |
| Story | "Last Tuesday, I saw $47,329 in one day." | Reader is in the scene |
| Confession | "I almost gave up three times." | Vulnerability disarms skepticism |
| Specific result | "In 9 months, we did $400k+." | Numbers create credibility |
| Question | "Ever stared at a blank page and froze?" | Matches their reality |
| Short sentence | "It's simple." | Zero friction to start |
| Math | "5 min + 10 min + 15 min = hours every week, forever." | Makes abstract concrete |

### Openings to AVOID (AI tells)
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Are you ready to take your business to the next level?"
- "Welcome! I'm so glad you're here."
- "Let's dive in!"

## Curiosity Gaps & Open Loops

Human brain craves closure. Open a loop = they keep reading to close it.

**Weak:** "10 Tips for Better Writing"
**Strong:** "I tested 47 headlines. One pattern beat everything by 3x."

### Techniques
1. **Incomplete story** — Start story, don't finish: "I discovered something... but first..."
2. **Numbered tease** — "One pattern beat everything. I'll show you in a moment..."
3. **Partial reveal** — "3 parts. First is obvious. Third is counterintuitive. But the second..."
4. **Seeds of curiosity** (Sugarman) — End paragraphs with: "But that's not the best part." / "Here's where it gets interesting." / "Which brings me to the real secret."

**Rules:** 2-4 per page. Must close every loop you open. Small loops: 1-3 paragraphs. Big loops: by end of piece.

---

## Flow: The Slippery Slide (Sugarman)

Every element pulls to the next. Reader can't stop.

### Bucket Brigades
Short transition phrases: And / So / Now / But / Look / Here's why / Truth is / Turns out / The result?

### Stutter Technique
Repeat a word from last sentence in first sentence of next paragraph.

### Short First Sentences
"It's simple." "Here's the problem." "This works."

### Vary Paragraph Length
Short. Then medium with more detail. Then short again. Creates rhythm.

### Momentum Killers
- Jargon requiring pause
- Long paragraphs without breaks
- Tangents disconnected from main thread
- Same sentence structure repeated

## Pain Quantification

Don't describe pain. Do the math:

> "4 hrs emails + 6 hrs landing page + 4 hrs webhooks + 2 hrs SEO + ∞ hrs overthinking = 22+ hours of headaches."

When readers see "22+ hours" they calculate if your price is worth it.

---

## The So What? Chain

For every feature, ask "so what?" 3 levels deep:

> Feature: Fast database → Queries in milliseconds → Users don't bounce, revenue doesn't leak → **You stop waking up stressed about churn**

Write from the bottom. Not "saves 4 hours" but "close your laptop at 5pm instead of 9pm."

---

## Rhythm: Alternation

Short sentence. Impact. Then a longer one that breathes, adds context, feels like conversation.

**Pattern:** Hook (short, sharp) → Expand (breathe, add context) → Land it (kicker that punctuates). Repeat.

---

## Founder Story (Highest-Trust Element)

Arc: **Vulnerability → Credibility → Shared Journey**

> "In 2018, I believed I was Mark Zuckerberg. Built a startup for 1 year. Got 0 users. After my burnout, I shipped like a madman — 16 startups in 2 years. Now I earn $45,000 a month."

Self-deprecation + specific failure + specific success = trust.

---

## Testimonials as Mini Case Studies

**Formula:** [Before state] + [Action] + [Specific outcome] + [Timeframe] + [Emotion]

- "I shipped in 6 days as a noob coder. Would have taken months. I wanna cry."
- "We bought our first business within 4 months of joining."

NOT: "Great product! Highly recommend."

---

## Disqualification (Velvet Rope)

Tell people they're NOT a fit:

> "You're NOT a good fit if you equate success with just buying a course."

Flips from "please buy" to "prove you're worthy." Pre-filters bad customers.

## CTAs

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "Sign Up" | "Get ShipFast" |
| "Learn More" | "See the exact template" |
| "Buy Now" | "Start building" |

Below CTA: **[Risk reversal] + [Social proof] + [Speed/ease]**
> "$199 once. Join 2,600+ marketers. 2 minutes to install."

## AI Tells to AVOID

### Words
delve, dive into, comprehensive, robust, cutting-edge, utilize, leverage, crucial, vital, essential, unlock, unleash, supercharge, game-changer, revolutionary, landscape, navigate, streamline

### Phrases
"In today's fast-paced world..." / "It's important to note..." / "When it comes to..." / "In order to..." / "Whether you're a... or a..." / "Let's dive in"

### Structural
- Same-length paragraphs
- Every bullet starts same way
- Too many headings
- Bold on every key term

### Voice
- Passive voice throughout
- No "I" or "you"
- Hedging: "some may find," "can potentially"
- No contractions
- Perfectly grammatical but lifeless

### Fix
Read aloud. Real humans use contractions, write fragments, have opinions, make unexpected word choices.

## Full Landing Page Sequence

1. **Hook** — Outcome headline with specific number/timeframe
2. **Problem** — Quantify the pain
3. **Agitate** — Scenario they recognize
4. **Credibility** — Founder story, authority, proof numbers
5. **Solution** — Transformation, not features
6. **Proof** — Specific-outcome testimonials
7. **Objections** — FAQ / fit-not-fit section
8. **Offer** — Price + value justification
9. **Urgency** — Only if authentic
10. **Final CTA** — Benefit-oriented + friction reducers

## Classic Frameworks Quick Reference

### Schwartz: 5 Levels of Awareness
1. **Unaware** → Lead with identity/emotion, long copy
2. **Problem-Aware** → Name problem vividly, introduce solutions
3. **Solution-Aware** → Show unique mechanism
4. **Product-Aware** → Overcome objections, add proof
5. **Most Aware** → Just make it easy (price, offer)

Less aware = longer copy. Most aware = short.

### Hopkins: Scientific Advertising
- Advertising = salesmanship in print
- Reason-why copy (explain WHY it works)
- Specificity creates believability
- Offer service, not salesmanship
- Test everything
- Spend 80% of time on headline

### Ogilvy: Confessions
- 5x more people read headline than body
- Show don't tell (specific detail > claim)
- Write the way you talk
- Tell the truth, but make it fascinating
- Give facts, not adjectives

### Halbert: Boron Letters
- Find a starving crowd first (offer > copy)
- Write to one person
- Tell stories, not pitches
- Handwrite great ads to internalize rhythm
- Read copy aloud
- AIDA: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action

### Caples: Tested Methods
- Headline = 80% of the ad
- Self-interest > cleverness
- Curiosity + benefit (not curiosity alone)
- One headline can outpull another by 19.5x

### Sugarman: Adweek Handbook
- Slippery slide (can't stop reading)
- Every element has one job (get to next element)
- Seeds of curiosity at paragraph endings
- 31 psychological triggers (honesty, proof, specificity, story, familiarity)
- Create the right buying environment

### Collier: Letter Book
- Enter the conversation already in their mind
- 6 essentials: attention, description, argument, urgency, risk-free, CTA
- Make them SEE themselves using the product
