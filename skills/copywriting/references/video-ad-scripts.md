# Video Ad Script Frameworks

Structured script frameworks for **talking-head / selfie video ads** on Meta (Facebook & Instagram). Designed for direct-response ads where the speaker talks directly to camera.

These frameworks complement the basic video timeline in `ad-creative-frameworks.md` with full A-to-Z script structures.

---

## Quick Reference: Which Framework When?

| Framework | Best For | Steps | Tone |
|-----------|----------|-------|------|
| **Straight-For-The-Kill** | Breaking beliefs, challenging common advice | 7 | Direct, confrontational |
| **Personal Story Ads** | Founder stories, transformation narratives | 9 | Relatable, authentic |
| **Don't Do That** | Contrarian positioning, new opportunity reveals | 11 | Pattern-interrupt, authoritative |

**Decision guide:**
- Audience believes something wrong → **Straight-For-The-Kill**
- You have a compelling personal/client transformation → **Personal Story**
- Common solutions are genuinely flawed and you offer something different → **Don't Do That**

# Framework 1: Straight-For-The-Kill

Designed to stop the scroll, break a belief, reveal the real problem, introduce a specific approach, and push one clear action.

## Structure

```
Hook → Myth → Hidden Problem → Unique Method → Proof (optional) → Outcome → CTA
```

## Step-by-Step Breakdown

### Step 1 — The Hook

The first line must **stop the scroll immediately** using either a direct audience call-out or a bold pattern interrupt.

The viewer must think: **"Wait… this is about me."**

**Audience call-out examples:**
- "Coaches posting content every day but still not signing clients…"
- "Gym owners struggling to get new members…"
- "Local businesses relying on referrals for leads…"

**Pattern interrupt examples:**
- "Posting more content will NOT grow your business."
- "Most marketing advice is keeping you broke."
- "Your ads aren't failing for the reason you think."

### Step 2 — The Myth (False Belief)

Identify what the market believes is the solution, then challenge it. Breaking a belief creates curiosity and attention.

- "You've probably been told you just need to post more content."
- "Most people will tell you the solution is running more ads."
- "You might think you just need better branding."

### Step 3 — Reveal the Real Hidden Problem

Explain what is actually causing the problem. This is the insight moment — the viewer should think: **"That actually makes sense."**

- "The real issue is your content never creates a buying moment."
- "The real problem is your ads are attracting the wrong people."
- "The real problem is your offer doesn't create urgency."

### Step 4 — Introduce the Unique Method

Introduce the approach that solves the problem. Keep it simple and clear — not a complicated strategy.

- "What you actually need is content designed to break beliefs."
- "You need a simple ad → landing page → booked call system."
- "You need messaging that creates tension and urgency."

### Step 5 — Optional Proof

A result, short example, or quick demonstration. Optional but useful.

- "That's how one video can bring in multiple booked calls."
- "That's exactly how our clients start getting consistent leads."
- "We've seen businesses go from zero inquiries to weekly bookings."

### Step 6 — Paint the Outcome

Show what life looks like after the fix. Help the viewer visualize the result.

- "That's when strangers start booking calls."
- "That's when leads start coming in every week."
- "That's when your pipeline finally becomes predictable."

### Step 7 — The CTA

One single action. No multiple options.

- "Click the link to see how it works."
- "Click the link to find out how to get consistent leads."
- "Click the link to learn how to build this system."

## Full Example (Coach Market)

> **Hook:** "Coaches posting content every day but still not signing clients…"
>
> **Myth:** "You've probably been told the answer is just posting more."
>
> **Hidden Problem:** "But that's not why you're not getting clients. The real issue is your content never creates a buying moment."
>
> **Unique Method:** "What you actually need is content that challenges beliefs."
>
> **Proof:** "That's how a single video can generate booked calls."
>
> **Outcome:** "Once you fix this, strangers start reaching out to work with you."
>
> **CTA:** "Click the link to find out how to do it."

## Key Rules

1. Short punchy lines
2. Conversational spoken language
3. Direct audience targeting
4. Break a belief
5. Reveal a mechanism
6. Paint the outcome
7. One CTA only

# Framework 2: Personal Story Ads

A direct-response storytelling structure for ads, webinars, VSLs, and social media. Turns a personal story into a persuasive ad script.

## Structure

```
Hook → Character → Limitation → Result → Mechanism → Barrier Removal → Audience Bridge → Offer → CTA
```

**Psychological flow:**

```
Attention → Relatable Story → Struggle → Transformation → Method → Believability → Relevance → Offer → Action
```

## Step-by-Step Breakdown

### 1. Hook (Pattern Interrupt)

Stop the viewer and make them curious with a surprising statement, confession, bold claim, or contradiction.

- "I almost quit my business 6 months ago."
- "This one mistake cost me $50,000."
- "Everyone told me this would never work."
- "I accidentally discovered a marketing trick that doubled my leads."

### 2. Character (Who the story is about)

Introduce the person — yourself, a student, or a client.

> "Two years ago I was just a freelance designer working out of my bedroom."

### 3. Limitation / Contrast (Why success seemed unlikely)

Stories without struggle aren't persuasive. Show lack of money, experience, failed attempts, doubt, obstacles.

> "I had no audience, no ads budget, and honestly no clue how marketing worked."

### 4. Result (Outcome achieved)

Reveal the transformation — revenue, leads, growth, lifestyle change, client wins. Must feel impressive relative to the starting point.

> "But within 90 days that same business was generating $42,000 in sales."

### 5. Mechanism (The system or method responsible)

The most important persuasion element. Don't say "I worked hard" — introduce a specific system or method.

> "The breakthrough came when I discovered a simple 3-step storytelling ad structure."

### 6. Barrier Removal (Why the viewer can do it too)

Remove excuses: no experience required, no big budget, no tech skills needed.

> "And the crazy part is you don't need a big audience or expensive ads to use it."

### 7. Audience Bridge (Connect the story to the viewer)

Shift from the story to the viewer.

> "So if you're a coach, consultant, or creator trying to get more clients online…"

### 8. Offer

Introduce the training, webinar, product, challenge, or program.

> "I put together a short free training where I break down this storytelling ad system."

### 9. CTA (Call To Action)

Tell the viewer what to do next.

> "Click the link below and I'll show you exactly how it works."

## Full Example (Standard Version)

> **Hook:** "Six months ago my ads were completely failing."
>
> **Character:** "I run a small online coaching business."
>
> **Limitation:** "I was spending money on ads but barely getting any leads."
>
> **Result:** "Then one change doubled my conversion rate."
>
> **Mechanism:** "I started using a simple personal story ad framework."
>
> **Barrier Removal:** "And it works even if you're not good at writing or marketing."
>
> **Audience Bridge:** "So if you're struggling to get clients online…"
>
> **Offer:** "I created a short free training explaining this framework."
>
> **CTA:** "Click the link and watch it now."

# Framework 3: Don't Do That

A persuasion structure for short-form and mid-length video ads (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube ads, VSL creatives). Uses contrarian positioning to interrupt and redirect.

## Structure

```
Desired Outcome → Pattern Interrupt → Problem with Current Methods → New Opportunity → Mechanism → Demand → Friction Removal → Objection Destruction → Speed Positioning → Risk Reduction → CTA
```

## Step-by-Step Breakdown

### 1. Desired Outcome or Aspiration

Start by stating the result the viewer wants. Creates immediate relevance.

- "Want to lose 20 pounds without starving yourself?"
- "Want more qualified leads without spending thousands on ads?"
- "Want to automate your business so it runs without you?"

Template: `Want to [desired result] without [painful sacrifice]?`

### 2. Don't Do That Pattern Interrupt

The signature move. Tell the viewer NOT to do the common solutions.

- "Don't start another diet."
- "Don't run Facebook ads."
- "Don't buy another expensive course."

Template:
```
Then don't [common solution #1].
And definitely don't [common solution #2].
```

### 3. Explain Why Current Methods Are Flawed

Explain why those common paths are slow, flawed, or ineffective.

- "Most diets destroy your metabolism."
- "Most ad agencies burn your budget testing."
- "Most courses just overwhelm you with information."

Template:
```
Because most [common solution] actually [problem].
They force you to [frustrating process],
and you still end up [bad outcome].
```

### 4. Introduce a New Opportunity

Reveal that a different path exists. This is the turning point.

- "Instead, there's a new way people are losing weight."
- "Instead, businesses are using this AI system."
- "Instead, creators are using this growth engine."

Template: `Instead, there's a new way to [achieve desired result].`

### 5. Simplify the Mechanism

Explain how it works in simple language. Make it feel easy and obvious.

- "It works by activating a natural fat-burning switch in your body."
- "It automatically finds buyers already searching for your service."
- "It builds your content and posts it for you."

Template:
```
It works by [simple mechanism explanation].
Which means you can [desired benefit].
```

### 6. Show Demand / Validation / Authority

Prove the opportunity is real. Build credibility.

- "Over 50,000 people are already using this."
- "Top marketers are switching to this system."
- "This strategy generated $120M in sales."

Template:
```
That's why [large group / authority] are already using it.
And it's helping them [specific result].
```

### 7. Remove Effort (Friction Removal)

Show what is already done for the user. Reduce perceived work.

- "The system builds the funnels for you."
- "The workouts and plan are already mapped out for you."
- "The AI writes the ads for you."

Template:
```
Everything is already done for you.
The [system/tool] handles the [hard parts].
```

### 8. Destroy Objections

Handle common objections before the viewer raises them.

- "You don't need experience."
- "You don't need tech skills."
- "You don't need a big budget."

Template:
```
You don't need [skill].
You don't need [resource].
You don't even need [experience].
```

### 9. Position As Fastest or Simplest Path

Frame the solution as the cleanest, fastest route.

- "This is the fastest way to launch a profitable store."
- "This is the simplest way to grow an audience."
- "This is the cleanest way to get clients."

Template: `It's simply the fastest way to [desired result].`

### 10. Reduce Risk

Lower the perceived downside.

- "You can test it in minutes."
- "There's no long-term commitment."
- "You can try it risk-free."

Template: `You can try it with [low risk condition].`

### 11. Call To Action

Required format: `Click the link to find out how you can [desired result].`

## Full Example (Fitness)

> **Desired Outcome:** "Want to lose stubborn belly fat without starving yourself?"
>
> **Pattern Interrupt:** "Then don't start another diet. And definitely don't spend hours doing cardio."
>
> **Problem with Current Methods:** "Most diets slow down your metabolism. And endless cardio just burns muscle instead of fat."
>
> **New Opportunity:** "Instead, there's a new way people are triggering fat loss automatically."
>
> **Mechanism:** "It works by activating a metabolic switch that tells your body to burn stored fat."
>
> **Demand:** "That's why thousands of people are already using it."
>
> **Friction Removal:** "The workouts and plan are already mapped out for you."
>
> **Objection Destruction:** "You don't need gym experience. You don't need a strict diet. And you don't need hours a day."
>
> **Speed Positioning:** "It's simply the fastest way to lose stubborn fat."
>
> **Risk Reduction:** "You can try it in just a few minutes."
>
> **CTA:** "Click the link to find out how you can lose stubborn belly fat."

## Pairing with AI Video Generation

These frameworks write what the person **SAYS** — the script, dialogue, and persuasion structure.

For the visual prompt (what the video **LOOKS LIKE**), pair with the `video-director` skill. It generates ready-to-paste prompts for Sora, Kling, and VEO covering camera work, lighting, environment, realism, and negative prompts.

**Workflow:**
1. Write the script here (choose Straight-For-The-Kill, Personal Story, or Don't Do That)
2. Feed the dialogue into the video-director prompt template (usually Street Interview, Podcast, or Shock Hook type)
3. Review the combined script + visual prompt through the HITL gate
4. Paste approved prompt into the AI video tool
