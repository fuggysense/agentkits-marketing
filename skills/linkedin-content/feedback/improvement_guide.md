# Post Performance Diagnosis

> When a post underperforms, use this flow to diagnose why and improve.

## Quick Diagnosis Flow

### Low Likes (<50)

**Check in order:**

1. **Was the author in the story?** If it's about someone else (thought leader, historical figure, industry trend), that's the problem. Rewrite with the author as protagonist.

2. **Were the first 2 lines emotional or informational?** If informational ("Here are 3 ways to..."), the hook failed. Rewrite opening with tension, conflict, or a specific emotional moment.

3. **Was anything at stake?** If the post is safe to publish — no risk to reputation, relationships, or comfort — it lacks Stakes. Ask: "What's the version of this that would make me nervous to post?"

4. **Was it framework-first?** Posts that lead with structure (numbered lists, frameworks, tips) before a story consistently underperform. Invert: story first, framework embedded within.

### Low Comments (<10% of likes)

5. **Did it end with a question?** Posts without an invitation to respond get read but not replied to. End with a genuine question that invites the reader's experience.

6. **Was it too polished?** Overly structured, grammatically perfect posts feel like broadcasts, not conversations. Add imperfection: fragments, conversational asides, self-corrections.

7. **Did it trigger self-reflection?** Comments come when readers see themselves in the post. Identity declarations ("This is who I am") invite "me too" responses. Advice ("Here's what you should do") doesn't.

### Low Shares (<2% of likes)

8. **Did it name something?** Posts get shared when they give language to something the reader couldn't articulate. "Wrong people vs bad people" is shareable because it's a useful distinction. Generic advice isn't.

9. **Was it specific enough to be useful but universal enough to apply?** The sweet spot: specific personal story + universal human truth.

## Common Failure Patterns

| Pattern | Symptom | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Wikipedia post | Informative but zero personal investment | Put yourself in the story |
| Guru post | Advice-heavy, positions author as teacher | Switch from teaching to sharing |
| Humble brag | Achievement wrapped in fake humility | Own the win directly or tell the struggle story |
| Engagement bait | Question without substance | Lead with value, question at the end |
| AI voice | Smooth, structured, uses banned vocabulary | Roughen it up, add fragments, use contractions |
| Safe post | Nothing at risk, comfortable to publish | Ask "what's the version that makes me nervous?" |

## Improvement Cycle

1. **Identify the weakest SIREN element** (score each 0-2)
2. **Go back to the story bank** for raw material to strengthen it
3. **If the story bank is empty for that element**, schedule another interview round
4. **Rewrite and re-score** — target 7+/10 for Tier 1 posts
5. **Track the delta** — did the rewrite perform better? Update learnings.
