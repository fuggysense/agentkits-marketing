# LinkedIn Algorithm — Deep Reference

> Last verified: 2026-03-12

## Content Classification Pipeline

Every post goes through a 3-stage classifier before any human sees it:

### Stage 1: Spam Filter
- Checks for: banned URLs, known spam patterns, engagement bait phrases, excessive hashtags (10+), tagged 20+ people
- **Result:** Spam → suppressed immediately, never enters feed

### Stage 2: Low vs High Quality Scoring

| Signal | Low Quality | High Quality |
|--------|------------|--------------|
| Format | Wall of text, no breaks | Structured, scannable, line breaks |
| Originality | Rephrased viral post | Original insight/experience |
| Engagement bait | "Like if you agree" | Genuine question inviting discussion |
| Links | External URL in post body | No link or link in comment |
| Hashtags | 6+ or irrelevant | 3-5, relevant to content |
| Media | None or stock imagery | Original image, carousel, native video |

### Stage 3: Creator Authority Score

LinkedIn weights your historical performance:
- Avg engagement rate over last 30 posts
- Profile completeness (SSI component)
- Network quality (are your connections active?)
- Topic consistency (do you post about same themes?)
- Comment quality (do people write substantive replies?)

Higher authority = larger initial test audience.

## 4 Distribution Phases

### Phase 1: Classification (0-15 minutes)
- Post enters spam/quality classifier
- If passes: assigned to initial test group
- Test group = ~8-10% of your 1st-degree connections
- **What matters:** Nothing you can do here — this is algorithmic

### Phase 2: Test Audience (15-60 minutes)
- Shown to test group
- Algorithm measures: dwell time, reactions, comments, shares, click-through
- **Critical window:** Early engagement in first hour determines trajectory
- **What matters:** Reply to EVERY comment immediately to boost signals

### Phase 3: Scoring & Expansion (1-8 hours)
- If test audience engagement exceeds your baseline → expand to broader network
- Shown to remaining 1st-degree + some 2nd-degree connections
- Algorithm compares your post's engagement rate to:
  - Your own average (personal baseline)
  - Similar content from similar creators (cohort baseline)
- **What matters:** Dwell time + comment depth are weighted highest here

### Phase 4: Broader / Long Tail (8 hours - 7 days)
- Top-performing content reaches 2nd and 3rd-degree connections
- Appears in hashtag feeds, topic feeds, LinkedIn Today
- Content can resurface days later if someone influential engages
- **What matters:** Evergreen value — posts that stay relevant get extended distribution

## Engagement Signal Weighting

Ranked by impact on distribution (highest to lowest):

| Rank | Signal | Weight | Why |
|------|--------|--------|-----|
| 1 | **Dwell time** | Highest | Proves content is worth reading — hardest to fake |
| 2 | **Comments (5+ words)** | Very high | Substantive discussion = high-quality content signal |
| 3 | **Shares with commentary** | High | Endorsement + new distribution |
| 4 | **Saves/bookmarks** | High | "I want to come back to this" = strong quality signal |
| 5 | **Reactions** | Medium | Easy engagement — lower signal than comments |
| 6 | **Click to "See more"** | Medium | Shows hook worked, but post must also retain |
| 7 | **Profile visits from post** | Medium | Reader wanted to learn about author |
| 8 | **Follow from post** | Medium-Low | Strong individual signal but rare |
| 9 | **Shares without commentary** | Low | Low-effort amplification |
| 10 | **Reactions on comments** | Low | Nested engagement, minor signal |

## Content Type Algorithm Preferences (2025-2026)

| Content Type | Organic Reach Potential | Algorithm Favor |
|-------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| Text-only posts | Very High | Most favored — highest dwell time |
| Carousels (PDF) | Very High | Strong — dwell time per slide counts |
| Native video | High | Growing — especially vertical format |
| Images + text | Medium-High | Depends on image originality |
| Newsletters | Medium (but direct to subscribers) | Bypasses algorithm for subscribers |
| Articles | Low | Minimal feed distribution |
| Polls | Low-Medium | Deprioritized in 2025 (overused) |
| External links | Low | 40-50% reach reduction vs no-link posts |
| Reposts without commentary | Very Low | Near-zero distribution |

## Negative Signals

Things that actively hurt your distribution:

| Signal | Impact | Detail |
|--------|--------|--------|
| External links in body | -40-50% reach | Algorithm assumes you're driving traffic away |
| Edit within first hour | -20-30% reach | Algorithm re-classifies the post |
| Delete and repost | Penalized | Algorithm remembers — treat as spam-like behavior |
| Engagement pods | Detection + suppression | Same group engaging on every post = pattern detection |
| Tag people who don't engage | Negative signal | Tagged people ignoring your post = low relevance |
| Posting 2x/day | 2nd post cannibalized | Algorithm deprioritizes the lower performer |
| Generic hashtags only | Reduced targeting | #motivation #success = too broad, no topic signal |
| Commenting on own post immediately | Neutral-negative | Perceived as gaming; wait 10-15 min |

## Timing Optimization

### Best posting times (general B2B audience):

| Priority | Time Slot | Day | Reason |
|----------|-----------|-----|--------|
| 1 | 7:30-8:30 AM | Tue-Thu | Morning commute/coffee scroll |
| 2 | 12:00-1:00 PM | Tue-Thu | Lunch break browsing |
| 3 | 5:00-6:00 PM | Tue-Wed | End-of-day wind-down |
| 4 | 8:00-9:00 AM | Mon | Week kickoff, less competition |
| 5 | 8:00-9:00 AM | Fri | Pre-weekend, lower engagement |

### Frequency guidelines:
- **Minimum:** 3x/week to stay in algorithm's active creator pool
- **Optimal:** 5x/week (weekdays)
- **Maximum effective:** 1x/day (2nd post in same day gets deprioritized)
- **Break tolerance:** Missing 1-2 days is fine; 7+ day gap = algorithm resets your baseline

### Post spacing:
- Wait minimum 18-24 hours between posts
- Posting twice in 12 hours = second post gets ~50% less distribution

## Algorithm Changes to Watch

LinkedIn's algorithm is shifting toward:
1. **Knowledge and advice** — Posts that teach something specific
2. **Personal experience** — First-person stories with lessons
3. **Niche expertise** — Specialist content over generalist
4. **Meaningful conversations** — Comment quality > comment quantity
5. **Video growth** — LinkedIn is investing in video, expect more reach for native video

**De-prioritizing:**
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree", "Comment YES for...")
- Viral recycling (rehashing trending posts)
- Follow/unfollow tactics
- Automated activity
