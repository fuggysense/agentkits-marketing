# Carrier Trust Signals

## Purpose
Step 8 of the offer-building process. Trust signals are the "carriers" that transport belief from you to the buyer. Without them, even a great offer falls flat.

---

## The 7 Trust Signals

Every offer needs a mix of these. Rate your current strength (0-3) for each, then build the weak ones.

### 1. Results Proof
**What:** Concrete outcomes from real customers
**Examples:**
- "Went from $3K/mo to $28K/mo in 90 days"
- Revenue screenshots, analytics dashboards
- Before/after comparisons with numbers

**Strength scale:**
- 0 = No customer results yet
- 1 = Anecdotal ("clients love it")
- 2 = Specific stories with some numbers
- 3 = Hard data, multiple cases, independently verifiable

### 2. Social Proof
**What:** Evidence that others have chosen and trusted you
**Examples:**
- Testimonials (video > text > anonymous)
- Customer count ("500+ companies trust us")
- Logos, badges, "as seen in" press mentions
- Community size, waitlist numbers

**Strength scale:**
- 0 = No testimonials or social evidence
- 1 = A few text testimonials
- 2 = Multiple testimonials with names/photos, some video
- 3 = Overwhelming proof — video testimonials, recognizable logos, large community

### 3. Authority Proof
**What:** Why YOU are qualified to deliver this
**Examples:**
- Years of experience in this specific domain
- Credentials, certifications, education
- Published work (books, articles, talks)
- Notable clients or projects

**Strength scale:**
- 0 = No domain authority established
- 1 = Some relevant experience
- 2 = Clear expertise with credentials or portfolio
- 3 = Recognized expert — published, speaking, teaching

### 4. Mechanism Proof
**What:** Evidence that your METHOD works, not just that you get results
**Examples:**
- Named methodology with clear steps
- Framework diagram or process map
- "Here's exactly how it works" walkthrough
- Proprietary tools or technology

**Strength scale:**
- 0 = No defined method — "I just figure it out"
- 1 = Informal process
- 2 = Named method with clear steps
- 3 = Proprietary system with documentation, possibly IP

### 5. Risk Reversal
**What:** What you do to make the purchase feel safe
**Examples:**
- Money-back guarantee (30, 60, 90 days)
- Performance guarantee ("or I work free until...")
- Free trial / pilot period
- Pay-for-results model

**Strength scale:**
- 0 = No guarantee — "all sales final"
- 1 = Vague promise ("satisfaction guaranteed")
- 2 = Specific guarantee with clear terms
- 3 = Bold, specific guarantee that signals extreme confidence

### 6. Specificity
**What:** How concrete and detailed your offer is (vague = untrustworthy)
**Examples:**
- Exact deliverables listed
- Timeline with milestones
- "You get X, Y, and Z" not "we'll work together"
- Pricing clarity — no hidden costs

**Strength scale:**
- 0 = Vague ("let's chat about what you need")
- 1 = General description of services
- 2 = Clear deliverables and timeline
- 3 = Exact scope, timeline, deliverables, and pricing — no ambiguity

### 7. Demonstration
**What:** Showing (not telling) that your thing works
**Examples:**
- Free content that delivers real value (not teasers)
- Live demos, walkthroughs, free audits
- Case study deep-dives
- Free tool or sample of the product

**Strength scale:**
- 0 = No demonstration — "trust me"
- 1 = Surface-level content
- 2 = Valuable free content that proves expertise
- 3 = Free resource so good it sells the paid offer by itself

## Minimum Viable Trust

Not every offer needs all 7 at maximum. But every offer needs:
- **At least 1 proof signal** (results, social, or authority) scoring 2+
- **At least 1 safety signal** (risk reversal or demonstration) scoring 2+
- **Specificity** scoring 2+ (always — vagueness kills conversion)

If you don't have these minimums, the offer isn't ready to deploy regardless of how strong the core offer is.
