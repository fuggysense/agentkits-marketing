# Raw Pour — Deep Discovery Questions

## Purpose
Step 1 of the offer-building process. These 10 questions extract the raw material for everything that follows. The goal is emotional, unfiltered truth — not polished marketing speak.

**Instructions:** Ask these one at a time. Let the user talk. Don't correct, edit, or optimize yet. This is a pour, not a polish.

---

## The 10 Questions

### Q1: Origin Story
"How did you get into this? What happened — the real version, not the LinkedIn version?"
- **What you're mining:** Founder-market fit, personal stakes, credibility source
- **Listen for:** Pain they experienced personally, "I couldn't find X so I built it"

### Q2: The Frustrated Buyer
"Describe the person who needs this the most. Not demographics — describe their Tuesday at 2pm. What's frustrating them right now?"
- **What you're mining:** Visceral ICP detail, emotional language, situational triggers
- **Listen for:** Specific moments, not abstract pain points

### Q3: The Broken Alternative
"What are they doing today instead of using your thing? Why does that suck?"
- **What you're mining:** Status quo pain, competitive landscape, switching motivation
- **Listen for:** Workarounds, duct-tape solutions, "they're using spreadsheets for..."

### Q4: The Transformation
"If your thing works perfectly, what does their life look like 90 days later? Be specific."
- **What you're mining:** Outcome framing, before/after contrast, success metrics
- **Listen for:** Concrete outcomes (revenue, time saved, feelings), not features

### Q5: The Proof
"Who's your best customer story? What happened — numbers, timeline, the whole thing?"
- **What you're mining:** Social proof, case study material, believable specifics
- **Listen for:** Numbers, timeframes, direct quotes, "before X, after Y"

### Q6: The Objection
"When someone says no, what's the real reason? Not the polite reason — the real one."
- **What you're mining:** Purchase barriers, trust gaps, positioning failures
- **Listen for:** Price sensitivity, skepticism ("I've tried things like this"), timing ("not now")

### Q7: The Unfair Advantage
"What do you know or have that a competitor can't easily copy?"
- **What you're mining:** Defensibility, unique mechanism, intellectual property
- **Listen for:** Proprietary process, unique data, specific expertise, relationships

### Q8: The Aha Moment
"When does a customer go from 'I'm interested' to 'I need this'? What tips them over?"
- **What you're mining:** Conversion triggers, activation events, urgency sources
- **Listen for:** Specific demos, trial moments, conversations, realizations

### Q9: The Guarantee Instinct
"If you had to guarantee a result, what would you feel comfortable guaranteeing?"
- **What you're mining:** Confidence level, risk reversal material, promise boundaries
- **Listen for:** What they'll stand behind vs. what makes them hedge

### Q10: The Price Anchor
"If this works perfectly, how much is that worth to the buyer? Not what you charge — what's the value?"
- **What you're mining:** Value-price gap, ROI framing, price justification
- **Listen for:** Revenue impact, cost savings, time value, opportunity cost

---

## Processing the Raw Pour

After all 10 answers are collected:
1. **Highlight emotionally charged phrases** — these become copy ammunition
2. **Identify the dominant pain archetype** (time, money, status, risk, effort)
3. **Flag gaps** — any question where the answer was thin or vague = area needing research
4. **Extract the "core promise"** — distill all 10 answers into one sentence: "I help [who] achieve [what] by [how], even if [objection]"

Store raw answers in working notes. They feed every subsequent step.
