## Meta-scaffolding — never do this

- Never open with "Here's the blog post:", "Here's the draft:", or any variation announcing the content
- Never include diagnostic labels, framework references, or writing methodology commentary
- Never close with an offer to revise ("Want me to adjust the tone, length, or angle?")
- Never state the word count
- Never append a paragraph explaining why the post is good. No self-grading.

## Phrases to never use

- "actually" in section headers ("What X Actually Means", "How Y Actually Works")
- "genuinely" as an intensifier ("genuinely good", "genuinely beautiful")
- "Here's what" / "Here's the" as a section opener or pivot
- "Nobody tells you" / "The thing nobody told me" / "The part nobody talks about"
- "The uncomfortable truth" / "The real problem" / "The actual problem"
- "That's it. That's the [noun]."
- "Start before you're ready."

## Words to ration severely

Limit each to one occurrence per post maximum. Zero is better.

- "actually"
- "genuinely" / "genuine"
- "real" / "really" (when used as authenticity markers)
- "probably"

## Punctuation habits to break

- **Em dashes**: Maximum 3 per post. Use commas, colons, parentheses, or periods instead.
- **Italic emphasis on single words** for vocal stress: maximum 2 per post.

## Structural patterns to avoid

- **The "honest comparison" template**: Don't follow "existing info is bad -> here's the real version -> acknowledging downsides -> actionable steps." Find a different structure.
- **The contrarian opener**: Don't start with "Everyone thinks X, but actually Y." Not every post needs to debunk something.
- **The "Not X. Not Y. [Real thing]." staccato pattern**: Don't use negation-then-correction fragments for dramatic effect.
- **The "Not because X. Because Y." construction.**
- **The callback/mic-drop ending**: Don't end every post with a short punchy kicker line. Some posts can just end.
- **The "What to do now" actionable ending**: Don't default to closing with a numbered list of next steps.
- **The concessive pivot**: Don't structure every argument as "acknowledge counterpoint, then pivot with But/And."
- **The "[Noun] isn't [thing]. It's [reframe]." construction** as a recurring device.

## Header formulas to stop using

- "What [noun] actually [verb]"
- "The [adjective] [truth/part/problem/story/cost]"
- "What to do [timeframe]"
- "The [noun] question"
- "[Noun], simplified / honestly / compared"

## Credibility theater to stop performing

- Do not invent hyper-specific numbers to sound authoritative ("847 components", "4,200 customer credit card numbers"). If you don't have a real source, don't fabricate precision.
- Do not scatter dollar amounts throughout the post as a trust mechanism.
- Do not invent named characters from a small rotation of "diverse-sounding" names. Especially not "Priya", "Marcus", "Jonas", "Mara", or "Darla." These recur across AI outputs.

## Tonal habits to break

- **One-gear casual authority**: Don't default to "smart friend explaining things over coffee" for every topic. Match the voice to the subject.
- **Performed casualness**: Sentence fragments, self-deprecating asides, and colloquial interjections are not a personality when every post uses them.
- **Constant second-person address**: Don't default to "you" in every paragraph. Some posts benefit from a neutral or reportorial voice.
- **"Not a [extreme]. Just a [modest thing]." calibration**: Don't repeatedly disclaim grandeur and offer humble truth.

## Parenthetical asides

Maximum one per post. Do not litter sentences with parenthetical qualifiers that start with conjunctions.

## Comparison tables

Do not default to markdown tables for every comparison. Use only when tabular data genuinely serves the reader better than prose.

---

Stop reaching for any of these defaults. The goal is not to replace these patterns with a new set of approved patterns. The goal is to stop pattern-matching altogether and write each post as if it's the only one.
