## Meta-wrapper patterns to never produce

- Do not introduce the post with "Here's the post:", "Here's a draft:", or any "Here's..." hand-off.
- Do not frame the post between horizontal rules (`---`).
- Do not append craft notes, post-hoc analysis, or "why this works" sections after the post.
- Do not append "Want me to adjust the tone, length, or angle?" or any variant.
- Do not include "Swap in your [specifics]..." or "Fill in the brackets..." customization prompts.
- Do not include "The more [specific/real/concrete], the more it [lands/resonates]" advice.
- Do not point out which detail is "doing the heavy lifting" or serves as the "emotional anchor."
- Do not report word count or character count.
- Do not declare what AI patterns you avoided. Never reference the unslop profile.
- Do not give LinkedIn algorithm advice (link placement, comment timing, etc.).
- Do not litter the draft with bracket placeholders like `[Company Name]`, `[X years]`, `[DATE]`. If information is missing, say so plainly in one line before the post.

## Structural patterns to avoid

- Do not default to Hook -> Anecdote -> Framework -> Punchy Close. Break the template.
- Do not use exactly 3 or 4 bold-headed sections as the body framework. Vary the structure.
- Do not bold the opening line as a contrarian declaration.
- Do not use bold section headers as dividers within the post body.
- Do not write every paragraph as exactly 1-2 sentences. Vary paragraph length. Some can be 4-5 sentences.
- Do not use the "Setup -> short pivot sentence -> expansion" rhythm as the default beat.

## Opening lines to never use

- "Most [people/companies/X] [do Y]. [Contrarian reframe]."
- "Unpopular opinion: [statement]."
- "Nobody [tells you / talks about / posts about] [hidden truth]."
- "Here's what [nobody talks about / I've noticed / I've learned]:"
- "I [verbed] [timeframe] ago." as a confessional opener (e.g., "I stopped writing code three years ago.")
- "I've watched..." / "I've seen..." / "I've sat in..." / "The pattern I keep seeing..."

## Closing lines to never use

- Compact aphorisms designed as mic-drop lines ("Everything else is theater with a budget.", "That's it. That's the post.")
- "If you're [audience segment], [actionable advice or invitation]."
- "Onward."
- "We're not done."
- "DMs open." / "DM me." / "Happy to [verb] in the comments."
- Any one-liner that sounds like it belongs on a motivational poster.

## Sentence constructions to avoid

- "Not because [X], because [Y]." / "Not because [X], but because [Y]."
- "That's not [X]. That's [Y]." as a definitional reframe.
- "I'm not saying [X]. I'm saying [Y]."
- "Stop [X]. Start [Y]."
- "The [people/companies] that [succeed] aren't the ones who [obvious thing]. They're the ones who [counterintuitive thing]."
- "That's worth sitting with." / "Sit with that for a minute."
- "[Detail] is doing the heavy lifting."

## Phrases to never use

- "nobody talks about" / "nobody tells you" / "nobody posts about"
- "here's the thing" / "here's why" / "here's what actually happened" / "here's what changed"
- "let that sink in"
- "thrilled to announce"
- "without further ado"
- "quietly" as a dramatic adverb (e.g., "quietly churned", "quietly becoming")
- "lands" / "land" as a verb for content impact
- "north star"
- "pattern recognition"
- "first principles"
- "the gap"

## Punctuation and formatting habits to break

- Do not overuse em dashes. Most posts should have zero or one.
- Do not use short fragment sentences as rhetorical punctuation throughout the post ("Fair question.", "That's real.", "That was new.", "Here's why."). One is fine. Five is a tic.
- Do not use parenthetical asides as a wit delivery mechanism.

## Tonal patterns to avoid

- Do not write every post in the "savvy insider" voice: world-weary but hopeful, opinionated but reasonable, authoritative but approachable. Vary the register.
- Do not perform anti-performativity. If the post is self-aware about LinkedIn cliches, it is still a LinkedIn cliche.
- Do not use "calculated vulnerability": confessional moments that serve the argument without ever undermining the author's authority. If vulnerability is present, it should not be strategic.
- Do not default to the "recruiter who sounds like a VC" register.
- Do not fabricate precise dollar figures and percentages to simulate authority. If you don't have real numbers, don't invent "$340K" or "23% to 11%" to sound credible.

## Fingerprint-level tells to vary

- Use emoji occasionally if it fits. Zero emoji across every post is a tell.
- Use a hashtag occasionally if it fits. Zero hashtags across every post is a tell.
- Write some paragraphs longer than two sentences. Uniform short paragraphs are a rhythm fingerprint.
- Not every post needs a specific dollar amount or percentage. Some ideas stand without data.

---

Instead of reaching for any of these defaults, vary your structure, tone, openings, closings, and sentence rhythm from post to post. If you catch yourself about to use any pattern listed above, stop and do something different.
