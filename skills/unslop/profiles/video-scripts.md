## Meta-scaffolding to eliminate

Never wrap scripts in deliverable packaging:

- No "Here's your script:" or any preamble before the script itself
- No word counts, WPM calculations, or runtime estimates
- No "Production notes:" / "Director's notes:" / "Delivery notes:" sections appended after
- No copywriting framework citations ("Primary master: Hopkins", "Collier opening")
- No awareness-level / market-sophistication headers
- No "Swap in your real numbers" / "Replace the bracketed sections" customization instructions

The script is the deliverable. Nothing before it, nothing after it.

## Structural patterns to stop using

- **Labeled funnel sections.** Do not use HOOK, PROBLEM, BRIDGE, PROOF, CTA, CLOSE as section headers. Write a script, not a framework diagram.
- **"COLD OPEN" as a labeled section.** If the script opens cold, just open cold.
- **Second-by-second timestamps.** Do not break scripts into "[0:00-0:02] HOOK" micro-segments. False precision.
- **"END CARD:" sections.** Don't append end card instructions as a labeled block.
- **Markdown horizontal rules (---) as section dividers.** The average AI script has 4+. Use none, or only at true act breaks.

## Stage directions to retire

- "CUT TO:" as the default transition between every scene
- "direct to camera" paired with "mid-thought energy" / "mid-conversation energy"
- "[Beat]" / "(beat)" as a dramatic pause direction
- "B-roll:" followed by a stock-footage shopping list
- "TEXT ON SCREEN:" / "Text overlay:" as a stage direction
- "Quick cuts" / "quick montage" as a blanket visual instruction
- "VO:" / "V.O.:" applied rigidly to informal formats (TikTok, Reels, casual YouTube)

## Phrases to never use

- "Here's the thing" / "Here's what" / "Here's where" / "Here's why" as a pivot
- "actually" as a casual-authority signal ("what your skin actually needs")
- "That's it." as a mic-drop sentence
- "Nobody" as a dramatic intensifier ("Nobody was watching", "nobody wants to say")
- "every single" ("every single morning", "every single year")
- "Not X, Y" reframing
- "Read that again."

## Sentence-level habits to break

- **Em dash addiction.** Max 2-3 per script.
- **"No X. No Y. No Z." negative triads.** One negation fine. Three stacked is a template.
- **Defining by negation before affirmation.** Don't spend 2-3 sentences saying what something ISN'T before saying what it IS.
- **Starting sentences with "And" or "But" to sound casual.** One or two is natural. Six is a tic.
- **Parenthetical stage directions simulating spontaneity** — "*(to camera, mid-thought)*"
- **The rule of three by default.** Not every list needs exactly three items.

## Tonal patterns to avoid

- **Performative anti-establishment voice.** Don't open by dismissing the conventional approach then presenting the "real" version.
- **"You've been doing it wrong" openings.** Stop telling the audience their current approach is flawed as the default hook.
- **False casualness.** Don't describe the tone in production notes instead of writing in that tone. Never write "Tone: like advice from a friend."
- **Hyper-specific numbers as credibility theater.** Don't invent "$4,217/mo" or "47 minutes" to simulate data-backed authority. Round numbers are fine.
- **The conveniently perfect anecdote.** Real stories have friction. If an anecdote resolves too cleanly, cut it.

## Closing patterns to stop

- **Philosophical one-liner closes.** Don't end with a pithy reframe that broadens into a life lesson.
- **The callback close** where the ending references the opening image. Not every script needs to rhyme with its beginning.
- **Subscribe / follow / link CTAs** tacked on as a section. If the user wants a CTA they will ask.

## Production note cliches to drop

If production notes are requested, do not default to:
- "Handheld camera where possible" / "no stock footage" / "real employees, not actors"
- "Lo-fi" / "soft acoustic" / "light piano" / "no swells" as default music direction
- "Should feel like GoPro footage" / "phone-quality" / "polished video kills trust"
- "Let B-roll breathe" / "Leave pauses in"
- "Conversational" and "warm" as tonal descriptors (28% and 20% of AI scripts respectively)

## Structural habits to break

- **The mid-script "honesty check."** Don't insert a counterargument at 60-70% that gets dismissed within 2-3 paragraphs.
- **Reveal-after-specifics.** Don't always stack details then deliver a short punchy interpretive sentence. Vary the rhythm.
- **Every anecdote proves the thesis.** Let some stories complicate the argument.
- **Clean resolution.** Not every script needs to resolve. Ambiguity and open questions are legitimate.

If a choice feels automatic, it's probably on this list. Do something else. Vary structure, openings, cadence, and closings.
